## 2. Web Accessibility / Standards Section

### WCAG Updates:

**WCAG 2.2 (October 2023)**
- URL: https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/
- Description: Latest W3C Recommendation (October 5, 2023) with 9 new success criteria improving accessibility for visual, cognitive, and motor disabilities. Official current standard. (Added 2025)
- Priority: CRITICAL

**What's New in WCAG 2.2**
- URL: https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/new-in-22/
- Description: Official W3C guide explaining the 9 new success criteria in WCAG 2.2, including Focus Not Obscured, Consistent Help, Redundant Entry, and authentication improvements. (Added 2025)
- Priority: HIGH

**WCAG 3.0 Working Draft**
- URL: https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/wcag3-intro/
- Description: Introduction to W3C Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 3.0, currently in Working Draft status. Not expected to be finalized for several years. WCAG 2.2 remains the official standard. (Added 2025)
- Priority: MEDIUM

**WCAG 2.2 Quick Reference**
- URL: https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/quickref/
- Description: Customizable quick reference to WCAG 2.2 success criteria, techniques, and understanding documents. Filter by level (A, AA, AAA) and technology. Official W3C resource. (Added 2025)
- Priority: HIGH

## 6. Compliance & Standards Section

### Legal and Regulatory Updates:

**European Accessibility Act (EAA) 2025**
- URL: https://accessible-eu-centre.ec.europa.eu/index_en
- Description: EU accessibility requirements for products and services, effective June 28, 2025. Requires EN 301 549 compliance (WCAG 2.1 Level AA). Enforcement varies by member state. Official EU resource. (Added 2025)
- Priority: CRITICAL

**ADA Title III Digital Accessibility**
- URL: https://www.ada.gov/resources/web-guidance/
- Description: Official DOJ guidance on web accessibility under ADA Title III for state/local governments and businesses. Updated 2024. (Already listed but enhance with "Updated 2024")
- Priority: ENHANCE EXISTING

**ADA Website Accessibility Lawsuits 2024-2025 Trends**
- URL: https://www.adatitleiii.com/2025/04/federal-court-website-accessibility-lawsuit-filings-continue-to-decrease-in-2024/
- Description: Analysis of 2024 ADA Title III lawsuit trends showing 4,187 digital accessibility lawsuits filed. Key insights on repeat lawsuits, overlay failures, and e-commerce targets. Industry analysis. (Added 2025)
- Priority: MEDIUM

**Section 508 Refresh (2017 - Still Current)**
- URL: https://www.section508.gov/manage/laws-and-policies/
- Description: IT accessibility laws and policies for U.S. federal agencies under Section 508 Rehabilitation Act. Harmonized with WCAG 2.0 Level AA. (Already listed but enhance)
- Priority: ENHANCE EXISTING

**CVAA (Communications and Video Accessibility Act)**
